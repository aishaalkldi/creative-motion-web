# RASQ — Pilot Feedback Questions

**Purpose:** Structured questions for clinicians and patients after a **controlled pilot** session.  
**When:** Within 24–48 hours of demo or first live week.  
**Record answers in:** `pilot-evidence-log.md` · `pilot-metrics-manual-tracker.md`

**Do not collect PHI** in shared logs. Use first names or internal IDs only if clinic policy allows.

### Core questions

1. **Usefulness (1–5)**  
   How useful was RASQ for your supported workflow this week?  
   _1 = not useful · 5 = very useful_

2. **Most-used area**  
   Which did you use most?  
   - Assessment  
   - Plan assignment  
   - Patient portal follow-up  
   - Results / review queue  
   - Patients list (operational badges, session activity)  
   - Dashboard / Pilot Attention Queue  

3. **Review queue & attention queue**  
   Did the **Review queue** or **Pilot Attention Queue** surface something you might have missed?  
   - Yes · No · Sometimes  
   _Optional note: what surfaced?_

4. **Session activity (Sprint W)**  
   Were **Sessions: N of M** and **Last session** on the patient list/profile clear and helpful for follow-up?  
   - Yes · Partially · No · Did not notice  
   Was **Operational status only** wording clear (not a clinical score)?  
   - Yes · No

5. **Time friction**  
   What took longer than expected? _(workflow step, setup, patient follow-up, language, etc.)_

6. **Continue pilot**  
   Would you use RASQ with another patient next week?  
   - Definitely · Maybe · No  
   _Why?_

7. **Single improvement**  
   What one change would most improve RASQ for your clinic? _(documentation or product)_

8. **Trust & clarity**  
   | Question | 1–5 |
   |----------|-----|
   | I understand RASQ does not diagnose or prescribe autonomously | |
   | Safety framing on patient portal was adequate | |
   | I knew when to contact the patient after a flag | |

9. **Recommend to colleague (NPS)**  
   How likely are you to recommend this **controlled pilot** to a colleague?  
   _0 = not at all · 10 = extremely likely_

### Usability pain points (clinician)

- What screen or step was most confusing?
- Arabic/English: any mismatch between assessment and portal?
- Mobile vs desktop issues for you or patients?
- Link delivery problems (assessment vs portal)?

### What confused the user (clinician self-report)

- What did **you** misunderstand on first use?
- What did **patients** ask about repeatedly?

## Patient feedback

Use Arabic block in `patient-guide-ar.md` context, or WhatsApp templates in `patient-feedback-message.md`.

### Core questions (Arabic-friendly)

1. هل فتحت الرابط بسهولة؟ (نعم / لا)  
2. هل فهمت التمارين؟ (نعم / جزئياً / لا)  
3. هل شعرت أن البرنامج من معالجك؟ (نعم / لا)  
4. هل عرفت **متى تتوقف** إذا شعرت بأعراض غير طبيعية؟ (نعم / جزئياً / لا)  
5. هل ستكمل الجلسة القادمة؟ (نعم / غير متأكد / لا)  
6. ما الشيء الواحد الذي أربكك؟ _(كلمة أو جملة قصيرة)_

### Trust & clarity (patient)

| Theme | Question |
|-------|----------|
| Personalisation | Did the program feel assigned by your therapist? |
| Safety | Did you see a stop/safety message? |
| Contact | Did you know how to contact the clinic if symptoms worsened? |
| Technology | Any problem with link, language, or phone browser? |

### Usability pain points (patient)

- Could not open link
- Exercises unclear
- Effort/pain step confusing
- Language or reading direction (RTL)
- Fear or uncertainty about continuing

### What confused the user (patient)

Record verbatim short quote when possible (no clinical details in shared docs).
